Top 5 Most Culturally Significant Websites of the Early 2000s

  1. Space Jam (1996)

    An iconic relic of early web design that remains completely unchanged. Its starry background, basic table layout, and planetary navigation wheel perfectly capture the experimental essence of the early internet era.

  2. Homestar Runner

    A massive flash-animation website that defined internet humor in the early 2000s. It featured interactive menus, games, and weekly cartoons like Strong Bad Emails that paved the way for modern independent web content.

  3. Wikipedia (2001)

    Launched at the turn of the century, Wikipedia completely revolutionized how humanity collects and accesses information. Its plain, hyperlink-dense layout proved that collaborative knowledge sharing didn't need flashy design to change the world.

  4. MySpace (2003)

    The definitive social network of the mid-2000s. It introduced an entire generation to basic HTML and CSS coding by allowing users to completely customize their profile backgrounds, music players, and layouts.

  5. Neopets (1999)

    A virtual pet website that became a massive community hub in the early 2000s. Beyond looking after digital animals, it taught young users foundational concepts of supply, demand, and virtual economies through its shop system.
